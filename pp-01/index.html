PT Serif

The PT Serif font has an interesting history as it pays homage to type faces invented by Peter the Great nearly 3 centuries ago.

Volkhov

The Volkhov font does not share the same rich history as PT Serif, but it does come from similar lineage being related to the serif typeface.

Why both are amazing together

Similar to the pairings of Univers and Glypha, both PT Serif and Volkhov share a serif design. Not only that, but they have design elements that are not as common in other serifs. Particularly, the shape of the lowercase b does not have a stem that reaches the bottom. There are some slight differences in them though that make them disticnt from one another. For example, in Volkhov the middle line of the e slightly curves up while it stays straight in PT Serif. Additionally, for the "k" in PT Serif, the middle connects with a line while the two sections stay separate in Volkhov. BTW if you haven't noticed already, all of the headings for this webpage have been in Volkhov while the paragraphs have been in PT Serif (except for the examples).
